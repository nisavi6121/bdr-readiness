User Guide

How to navigate and use the BDR Readiness Score tool

What This Tool Does

The BDR Readiness Score replaces the binary MQL flag with a transparent 0–100 readiness score for every lead and contact in the CRM. Instead of working a flat list by arrival time, BDRs see a ranked queue with clear action guidance and an explanation for every score.

The Queue

The main view at / shows all records ranked by readiness score. The top metrics bar gives a snapshot of the current filtered population.

Tier badges

  • Call Now — high readiness (Lead ≥65, Contact ≥70). Priority outreach within 24 hours.
  • Follow Up — moderate readiness (Lead ≥35, Contact ≥40). Schedule within 5 business days.
  • Nurture — low readiness. Add to long-term sequence, no immediate outreach warranted.
  • Flagged — hard blocker present. Review before any outreach. Do not call.

Action badges

  • Call / Email — priority outreach, all channels open
  • Call only — opted out of email; use phone or event only
  • Follow Up — schedule a follow-up call or email
  • Phone / LinkedIn — email bounced; use alternative channels
  • Blocked — hard blocker, review before touching
  • Nurture — add to long-term sequence
  • Converted — lead was converted to a contact; work the contact record instead

Columns

  • Score — 0–100 readiness score with a visual bar. Higher is more ready.
  • Confidence — High / Medium / Low based on number of DQ flags on the record. High = 0–1 flags, Medium = 2–3, Low = 4+.
  • Seg. — account data segment (L1/L2/L3 for leads, C1/C2/C3 for contacts). Reflects data source confidence, not prospect quality.
  • 30d eng. — number of genuine (non-automated) engagement events in the last 30 days.
  • Last eng. — days since most recent engagement. "—" means no engagement history.
  • DQ — count of data quality flags. Amber if any flags present.

Sorting

Click any column header to sort. Click again to reverse direction. Default sort is by tier then score descending — this surfaces the highest-readiness records first within each tier.

Filters

  • Tier — filter to a single prioritisation tier
  • Entity type — show only Leads or only Contacts
  • Industry — filter by company industry
  • Min score — show only records above a score threshold
  • Action — filter by action tag (e.g. show only "Call / Email")
  • Named accounts only — restrict to manually curated strategic target accounts

Hit Apply to apply filters, Reset to clear all back to defaults. Filters stack — you can combine tier + industry + min score simultaneously.

Converted records

Rows shown at reduced opacity with a converted tag are leads that have been converted to a contact. The contact is the canonical record — do not action the lead. The linked contact record will appear in the queue at the appropriate tier.

Record Detail

Click any row to open the full record view. This shows:

Profile card

Contact details, job level, persona, company, industry, employee count, lead source, and country. Fields shown in amber are missing (data quality gap).

Score breakdown

The three components that make up the final score, each on a 0–100 scale with their respective weights:

  • Engagement — weighted recent campaign activity (leads 75%, contacts 60%)
  • Account Fit — ICP industry, named account, employee size, intent score adjusted by segment multiplier (leads 15%, contacts 25%)
  • Profile Fit — job level and job persona scores averaged (leads 10%, contacts 15%)

A sunburst chart shows how each component contributes to the final score. Hover segments for exact values.

BDR action

The recommended outreach action with any relevant soft flag notes (opted out, bounced email, automation-inflated engagement). This is the primary field BDRs should act on.

Data quality flags

Any DQ issues on the record are listed here. Flags affect the Confidence rating but do not directly reduce the score — a high-readiness record with a missing phone is still high readiness, just harder to reach.

Engagement history

Full campaign membership history sorted by response date descending. Each row shows the campaign name, type, status, and date. Automated rows (status = Sent) are shown at reduced opacity — they do not contribute to the engagement score.

Understanding the Score

Why is this record scored high?

Look at the score breakdown bars. A high engagement score (dark blue) with recent 30d activity is the strongest driver. Check the score explanation line on the record page for a plain-English summary.

Why is a strong-looking profile scored low?

Profile fit (job level + persona) contributes only 10–15% of the final score. A CISO at a Fortune 500 with zero engagement will score low — the model rewards current buying signals, not static profile quality. Check the "Last eng." column: if it's blank or 365+, that's why.

Why does a record show Flagged despite a high score?

Hard blockers (Non-Prospect persona, account do-not-contact, no longer with company) override the score entirely and assign the Flagged tier. The score is still computed and shown for reference but should not drive outreach.

What does confidence mean?

Confidence reflects data completeness, not score accuracy. A Low-confidence record has 4+ data quality flags (missing fields, duplicate email, broken links). The score is less reliable because it was computed on incomplete data.
